Carrot Daikon Slaw Pickle

Shredding a classic salad in brine form offers a double crunch with ginger zing. Ferment for 4 days for tang, then store chilled to top barbecue or fold into leafy greens within 2 weeks.

Ingredients:
1 lb. daikon radish, shredded
3 medium carrots, shredded
1 tbsp shredded peeled ginger
1 tbsp fine sea salt

Brine:
1 1⁄2 cups rice vinegar
1 cup water
2 tbsp organic cane sugar
1 tbsp sesame oil

Instructions:
Toss the shredded daikon, carrots, and ginger with salt, then pack them into a 1-quart mason jar.

Whisk or stir the brine ingredients in a bowl or measuring cup thoroughly until the sugar fully dissolves and the brine is well blended.

Pour the brine solution over the vegetables, leaving 1⁄2 inch headspace.

Seal the jar, then vigorously shake it for 30 seconds to eliminate air pockets.

Open the jar daily to release accumulated gas during the 4-day room temperature fermentation until bubbles cease forming.

Transfer the jar to the fridge to pause fermenting. Use pickled slaw within 2 weeks.
