Jiao zi

INGREDIENTS:

- 1 head Chinese cabbage, large
- salt
- 1 pound ground pork
- 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 1 bunch green onions
- 1/2 teaspoon ginger , finely chopped
- 3 cups water
- 1 package jiaozi skins, (potsticker wraps)
- water
- sesame oil
  Dipping sauce
- 8 tablespoons white vinegar
- 8 tablespoons soy sauce
- garlic, chopped
- (null) sesame oil


METHOD:

1. Chop cabbage very fine.  Sprinkle with salt and put in bowl.  Let sit for 15 minutes.  Wring water our of cabbage Place cabbage in a small bowl.  Add cabbage, pork, sesame oil, soy sauce, onions, and ginger. Mix Thoroughly.  Place one tsp. of the mixture in the center of jiaozi skin.  Moisten edge of jiaozi skin and press together to form a dumpling.  Cover dumplings with dry towel to keep moist until cooked.

2. To Cook:

3. If you DO NOT HAVE STEAMERS, follow the directions below.  If you DO have steamers, steam for 8 minutes under a pan of boiling water.

4. Bring large pan of water to a boil.  Drop in 20-25 Chiao Tzus.  When water comes to a boil, add one cup of cold water.  Bring to a third boil, add 1 cup of cold water.  Bring to a fourth boil, and remove water.  Place on plate and lightly sprinkle with sesame oil to keep from sticking.

5. This recipe makes 20-25.  When we make these in MY family, We quadruple the recipe.

6. For the dipping sauce: Mix together vinegar, soy sauce, and a few drops of sesame oil.  Add chopped garlic to taste.SOURCE: James Hall
