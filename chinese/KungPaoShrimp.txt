Kung Pao Shrimp
serves 2-4

Sauce
2T water
1T soy sauce
1T shaoxing wine
1T Chinkiang vinegar
1T sugar
1t sesame oil
1t cornstarch
pinch of white pepper

Stir-fry
3T neutral oil
10 small dried red chilies
1t sichuan peppercorns
2t minced garlic
2t minced fresh ginger
1/2 red bell pepper cut into 1/2" pieces
1/2 green bell pepper cut into 1/2" pieces
2 celery stalks, cut into 1/2" pieces
1 lb shrimp, shelled and deveined
kosher salt
2 scallions cut into 1" pieces
1/2c roasted unsalted peanuts

Make the sauce, whisk everything until cornstarch is dissolved.

Heat 2 T oil in a wok over high heat. Add chilies and peppercorns and stir-fry until they puff and brown slightly, about 5 seconds. Add the garlic, ginger, and bell peppers and fry until the peppers are browned in spots and crisp-tender, about 1 minute. Transfer to a plate. Add celery to the pan and fry until heated through and charred in spots, about 2 minutes, add to peppers.

Add the remaining 1T oil to the wok. Season the shrimp with salt and add to the wok. Fry until almost cooked, about 3 minutes. Return the peppers, celery, and spices to the wok. Add scallions and peanuts and toss to combine. Add sauce and cook, stirring until it bubbles and thickens. When the sauce is thick and the shrimp is cooked completely, remove from heat. Serve with rice.
