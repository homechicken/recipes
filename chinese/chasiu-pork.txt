Ingredients

Pork
	•	1.5 kg pork collar (or pork shoulder butt), trimmed slightly
	•	Cut into 4 equal pieces (about 1¼ in / 3 cm thick)

Marinade
	•	¼ cup light soy sauce -200g
	•	¼ cup hoisin sauce -150g
	•	2 tbsp oyster sauce
	•	2 tbsp Chinese rose wine (or Shaoxing wine if needed)
	•	1 tbsp toasted sesame oil
	•	½ tsp white pepper
	•	1 tsp five-spice powder
	•	A pinch of pink curing salt (optional but improves color)
	•	15 g (about 3 cloves) garlic, smashed
	•	15 g ginger, sliced
	•	1 tbsp red yeast rice powder (for natural red color)
	•	1 cube red fermented tofu (plus a little of the brine)

Glaze
	•	275 g of the reserved marinade
	•	2–4 tbsp maltose (or substitute with honey if needed, but texture differs)

⸻

Instructions

1. Prep the Pork
	1.	Lightly trim the pork but keep most of the fat.
	2.	Cut into 4 long strips of equal thickness.
	3.	Place each piece into a resealable bag.

⸻

2. Make the Marinade
	1.	Blend all marinade ingredients until smooth.
	2.	Reserve 275 g (about 1 cup) for the glaze.
	3.	Divide the remaining marinade among the bags — about 200 g per bag.
	4.	Seal bags and massage the pork to coat evenly.
	5.	Marinate 12–36 hours in the fridge.

⸻

3. Slow Roast
	1.	Heat oven to 325°F (163°C).
	2.	Transfer pork and marinade into a 9×13-inch baking pan.
	3.	Cover tightly with foil.
	4.	Roast 30 minutes.

Reduce heat and continue
	5.	Lower the oven to 225°F (107°C).
	6.	Roast 3–4 hours, until:
	•	A fork goes in with light resistance
	•	Pork is tender but not falling apart
	•	Leaner pieces may finish early — remove them if done
	7.	Let pork cool at least 15 minutes.

⸻

4. Make the Glaze
	1.	Strain the reserved marinade through a fine mesh sieve.
	2.	Add maltose. Wet your fingers with cold water to handle it if needed.
	3.	Warm mixture in a pot over medium heat until smooth and glossy.

⸻

5. Broil to Finish
	1.	Place pork pieces on a sheet pan.
	2.	Brush generously with glaze (or dip pieces directly into the hot glaze).
	3.	Position pan about 4 inches from the broiler.
	4.	Broil 6 minutes, until lightly charred at the edges.
	5.	Flip, glaze again if needed, and broil another ~6 minutes.
	6.	Rest 5–15 minutes before slicing.

⸻

6. Serve
	•	Slice into long strips.
	•	Serve over rice, in noodles, or with vegetables.
	•	Enjoy both lean and fatty pieces — each has a different texture.
